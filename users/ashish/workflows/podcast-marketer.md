# Podcast Marketing Workflow

## Overview

This workflow transforms podcast transcripts into compelling marketing assets for podcast launches. It generates LinkedIn posts optimized for engagement and identifies viral-worthy clip segments for social media distribution.

**Purpose:** Convert raw podcast transcripts into polished marketing materials that drive audience growth and engagement.

**Best for:** Podcast hosts, marketing teams, and content creators looking to maximize the reach of their podcast episodes.

## Input

### Required:
- **Podcast Transcript**: VTT file format from Zoom recordings (zoom/*.vtt)
  - Contains timestamps, speaker labels, and dialogue
  - Typically auto-generated by Zoom or transcription services

### Optional:
- **Speaker Background**: Information about the guest speaker
  - Professional bio, company details, notable achievements
  - Recent projects, publications, or initiatives
  - LinkedIn profile or website URL
- **Company Information**: Context about the guest's organization
  - Company mission, products, recent news
  - Target audience and market position
  - Key messaging or brand guidelines

**Pro Tip:** The more context you provide about the speaker and their company, the more tailored and authentic the marketing content will be.

## Output

This workflow generates two types of marketing assets:

### 1. LinkedIn Posts (4 Variations)
- **Hook-driven narrative posts** written from the podcast host's perspective
- Each post features a strong opening sentence designed to stop scrolling
- No em dashes (clean, modern punctuation style)
- Different angles/themes to test which resonates best with your audience
- Optimized length (150-250 words) for LinkedIn engagement

### 2. Short Clip Segments (5 Selections)
- **Under 60 seconds each** - perfect for social media shorts
- Surprising, punchy, or highly insightful moments
- Self-contained segments that work as standalone content
- Includes exact timestamps from VTT file for easy editing
- Optimized for viral potential and shareability

## Workflow Steps

### Step 1: Prepare Your Transcript

1. Locate your Zoom VTT transcript file (usually in zoom/*.vtt)
2. Review the file to ensure:
   - Timestamps are present and accurate
   - Speaker labels are clear
   - No major transcription errors in key segments
3. Gather any optional speaker/company background materials

### Step 2: Process with LLM Prompts

Use the four prompts below in sequence. Each prompt builds on insights from the previous steps.

### Step 3: Save Your Generated Content

**LinkedIn Posts:**
- Save to: `users/{username}/outputs/linkedin_posts_{episode_name}.md`
- Format: One markdown file with all 4 variations clearly labeled

**Clip Segments:**
- Save to: `users/{username}/outputs/clip_segments_{episode_name}.md`
- Include: Timestamps, duration, description, and suggested caption for each clip

## LLM Prompts for Marketing Content Generation

### Prompt 1: Analyze Transcript and Extract Key Themes

```
You are a podcast marketing strategist analyzing a podcast episode to identify the most compelling themes and moments.

Your task: Read the provided podcast transcript and extract:

1. **Main Themes** (3-5 major topics discussed)
   - Include brief context for each theme
   - Note which segments were most engaging or surprising

2. **Guest Expertise Areas**
   - What unique insights or experiences does the guest bring?
   - What makes them credible or interesting?

3. **Standout Quotes** (10-15 powerful quotes)
   - Look for statements that are:
     * Surprising or counterintuitive
     * Emotionally resonant
     * Actionable or practical
     * Controversial or debate-worthy
   - Include timestamp for each quote

4. **Narrative Arc**
   - What's the story of this conversation?
   - How does it progress from beginning to end?
   - What transformation or insight occurs?

5. **Target Audience Signals**
   - Who would benefit most from this content?
   - What problems or pain points are addressed?

Requirements:
- Be specific and cite timestamps
- Focus on marketing potential, not just accuracy
- Think about what would make someone want to listen
- Identify both educational and entertainment value

Optional context about the speaker/company:
[PASTE SPEAKER/COMPANY BACKGROUND HERE IF AVAILABLE]

Here's the podcast transcript:
[PASTE VTT TRANSCRIPT HERE]
```

**Expected Output:** A structured analysis document with themes, quotes, and marketing angles that will inform the content creation in subsequent prompts.

## Troubleshooting

### Common Issues and Solutions

**Problem: LinkedIn posts feel too sales-y or promotional**
- Solution: Focus on insights and value first, mention the episode second. Lead with "Here's what I learned..." rather than "Check out my new episode..."

**Problem: Can't find clip-worthy moments in the transcript**
- Solution: Look for:
  - Moments where the guest's energy increases
  - Times when you (the host) react with surprise
  - Practical how-to explanations
  - Personal stories or vulnerable moments
  - Disagreements or debates

**Problem: Hooks feel forced or clickbait-y**
- Solution: Use the "curiosity gap" technique - hint at valuable information without fully revealing it. Example: "The most successful founders do something counterintuitive..." (then explain what)

**Problem: VTT timestamps are inaccurate**
- Solution:
  - Watch/listen to the sections to verify
  - Zoom transcripts can be off by a few seconds
  - Adjust timestamps manually if needed
  - Add buffer (start 2-3 seconds early, end 2-3 seconds late)

**Problem: All 4 LinkedIn variations sound too similar**
- Solution: Force yourself to use different starting points:
  - Start with a question vs. a statement
  - Start with the guest's background vs. a key insight
  - Start with industry context vs. personal story
  - Use different tones (inspirational, educational, provocative, personal)

**Problem: Clips are too long (over 60 seconds)**
- Solution:
  - Find the core 20-30 seconds and add minimal context
  - Cut the setup and start mid-thought if needed
  - End before the full explanation - leave them wanting more
  - Split into two separate clips if both parts are strong

**Problem: Guest mentions competitors or sensitive topics**
- Solution:
  - Select different clip segments that avoid those mentions
  - Edit LinkedIn posts to focus on universal insights rather than specific companies
  - Use generic descriptions ("leading companies" instead of naming competitors)

## Advanced Tips

### Getting Better Results

1. **Provide Rich Context:** The more you tell the LLM about your guest, audience, and brand, the better the output. Include:
   - Your podcast's typical listener demographic
   - Your brand voice guidelines
   - Previous successful posts for reference
   - Your goals for this particular episode

2. **Iterate on Hooks:** If the first sentence hooks feel weak, ask for 10 alternative opening lines. Test different approaches:
   - Question format: "What if everything you know about X is wrong?"
   - Provocative statement: "Most people are doing X backwards."
   - Personal observation: "I've interviewed 100 founders. Only 3 did this."
   - Specific detail: "It took Sarah 847 customer observation hours to find this insight."

3. **Customize for Your Voice:** After getting initial output, refine with prompts like:
   - "Make this sound more conversational and less polished"
   - "Add more specific details from the conversation"
   - "Rewrite this with shorter sentences and more energy"
   - "Remove any jargon and make it accessible to non-experts"

4. **Test Clip Pacing:** When selecting clips, read them out loud or time them. Social media clips feel different than they look on paper. What seems punchy in text might feel rushed or slow when spoken.

5. **Seasonal and Trending Connections:** If the episode content relates to current events, trends, or seasonal topics, explicitly mention this in the prompts to get more timely, relevant marketing angles.

### Workflow Variations

**For Video Podcasts:**
- Add visual cues to clip selection (gestures, facial expressions, whiteboard moments)
- Consider thumbnail potential when selecting clips
- Note any visual elements that should be highlighted in captions

**For Multi-Guest Episodes:**
- Create separate LinkedIn posts for moments with different guests
- Tag both guests when relevant
- Select clips that show interesting interactions between guests

**For Technical/Educational Content:**
- Balance accessible hooks with specific technical details
- Create "101" clips for beginners and "advanced" clips for experts
- Use LinkedIn for professional audience, other platforms for broader reach

**For Series or Themed Episodes:**
- Reference previous episodes in the series in LinkedIn posts
- Create compilation clips that show evolution across episodes
- Use consistent hashtags for series discoverability

## Notes

- **Turnaround Time:** This workflow typically takes 30-45 minutes to complete once you have the transcript.
- **Batch Processing:** If you have multiple episodes, process them together to maintain consistency.
- **A/B Testing:** Post different LinkedIn variations at different times to see which angle performs best with your audience.
- **Repurposing:** These clips and posts can be repurposed for newsletters, blog posts, and email campaigns.
- **Guest Approval:** Consider sending LinkedIn posts to guests for review before posting, especially if including sensitive company information.
