## 1. Main Themes

### Theme 1: Offense vs. Defense Advantage in AI Security
- **Context:** Adversaries currently have the speed advantage - no legal reviews, governance, or compliance to slow them down
- **Key Insight:** "We have to be right all of the time and they just have to be right once"
- **Nuance:** Sandy believes this is temporary - defenders have resource advantages through collaboration (OWASP) and long-term orchestration potential
- **Most Engaging Segment:** Opening discussion about whether offense or defense is winning

### Theme 2: AI for Traditional Attacks vs. Attacking AI Models
- **Context:** The "sexy" perception is attacking AI models, but the reality is attackers use AI to enhance traditional attack workflows
- **Key Insight:** Attackers are using AI for recon, vulnerability research, code conversion - compressing the attack cycle
- **Critical Quote:** "Attacking the model may seem sexy and lucrative, but using the model for doing traditional attacks is sexier"
- **Most Engaging Segment:** Discussion about where real threats are vs. theoretical risks

### Theme 3: The Statistical Nature of AI Security
- **Context:** Unlike traditional binary security metrics, AI security requires statistical analysis
- **Key Insight:** "It's mathematically impossible to do AI governance at the core level" - the attack surface is too vast
- **Surprising Element:** Security has always been statistical, but we weren't honest about it
- **Most Engaging Segment:** Discussion about testing non-deterministic systems 200+ times

### Theme 4: Supply Chain Security and Code Complexity
- **Context:** npm attacks, CrowdStrike compromise demonstrate supply chain vulnerabilities
- **Key Insight:** The "Lego nature" of code makes tracking dependencies exponentially difficult
- **Real-World Example:** Log4j patching wasn't a simple update - required finding third and fourth layer dependencies
- **Most Engaging Segment:** Sandy's call to redefine asset management to include entire supply chains

### Theme 5: Board Communication and Risk Management
- **Context:** CISOs struggle to communicate AI security risk to boards
- **Key Insight:** Boards need the "Smoky the Bear sign" - simple risk indicators
- **Action Item:** Either accept the risk or invest - binary decision after statistical analysis
- **Most Engaging Segment:** Discussion about presenting risk as investment decisions, not negative reports
