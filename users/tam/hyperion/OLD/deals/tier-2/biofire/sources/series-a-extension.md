## Raw Content Summary

**Announcement - February 5, 2024**

Biofire, a Broomfield, Colorado-based defense tech startup developing biometric-enabled firearms, extended its Series A funding round to reach $22 million total with a $7 million extension. New investors include CAZ Investments and Valhalla Ventures, with participation from lead investor Founders Fund.

**Product**:
Biofire developed a handgun with fully integrated electronic fire-control system and biometrics that authenticates authorized users while remaining secure from unauthorized access. The Smart Gun launched for pre-order in the United States during 2023.

**Production Timeline**:
The company plans to ship first Smart Gun units to consumers in 2024. The new funding will accelerate initial production efforts.

**Significance**:
The extension 15 months after initial Series A close suggests investor confidence in the company's progress. Founders Fund's continued participation as lead investor signals conviction in Biofire's technology and market potential. The fact that Biofire subsequently delivered first shipments in August 2024 validates the timeline commitment and demonstrates execution capability.

**Investment Context**:
Total $22M Series A positions Biofire well for initial production ramp, but consumer hardware companies typically require $30M-$100M to reach volume manufacturing, suggesting Series B likely in 12-18 months.
