## Raw Content

**Headline**: Aiming to enhance the performance of quantum processors, US/Ukrainian startup Haiqu raises $4 million

**Published**: June 6, 2023

Haiqu, a US/Ukrainian startup with operations split between Lviv and San Francisco, has raised $4 million in a pre-seed funding round. The company focuses on developing software solutions to enhance quantum processor performance.

The startup was formed and incubated in fall 2022 through the Creative Destruction Lab Quantum stream in Toronto. Co-founders include Richard Givhan (CEO), a Stanford alumnus and former Entrepreneur-in-Residence at Mitsubishi Electric, and Mykola Maksymenko (CTO), previously a researcher at the Max Planck Society and Weizmann Institute of Science and former head of R&D at SoftServe Inc.

Haiqu describes its solution as "careful software for clumsy machines"—essentially a software layer designed to optimize current quantum hardware performance. The company leverages expertise in quantum complexity, artificial intelligence, and high-performance computing. The focus addresses a critical challenge: qubit instability and computational errors that have hindered quantum computing progress.

The $4 million pre-seed round was led by MaC Venture Capital, with participation from Toyota Ventures, SOMA Capital, u.ventures, SID Venture Partners, and Roosh Ventures. Angel investors include Paul Holland, Alexi Kirilenko, and Gordy Holterman. The funds will support R&D and go-to-market activities.

Toyota Ventures' Jim Adler noted: "Haiqu caught our attention with their optimization software that boosts quantum hardware to solve practical industry problems."

The founders emphasize urgency in bringing complex technology to market and getting their solutions into users' hands quickly.
