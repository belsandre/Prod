# Dirac: Manufacturing Documentation Automation Platform

## Company Overview
Dirac develops BuildOS, an AI-powered platform automating work instruction creation for complex mechanical assemblies. The platform generates animated, interactive assembly guides directly from CAD files, addressing what the company characterizes as "archaic and manual" manufacturing documentation processes.

## Funding Details
- **Round**: Seed funding of $10.7M
- **Investors**: Founders Fund (led by Trae Stephens) and Coatue (led by Thomas Laffont)
- **Location**: Empire State Building, New York

## Product Capabilities
BuildOS offers:
- Automatic generation of 3D animated work instructions from CAD uploads
- AI-suggested assembly sequences with customizable reordering
- Auto-updating visuals when engineers adjust parameters
- Shop-floor deployment with real-time instruction updates
- Integration with Siemens Teamcenter for enterprise deployment

The platform reportedly reduces work instruction creation from "weeks or months" to "hours or minutes."

## Customer Traction
- Early aerospace customer Ancra Aircraft achieved "95% time savings"
- Strategic partnership with Siemens for integration and distribution

## Founder Background
CEO Fil Aronshtein brings manufacturing experience from Northrop Grumman. The founding team includes engineers with aerospace, automotive, and submarine manufacturing backgrounds.

## Market Positioning
- **Target Industries**: Aerospace, defense, automotive OEMs, agriculture, construction equipment
- **TAM**: Approximately $20B (benchmarked against CAD market size)
- **Business Model**: SaaS licensing per seat, site, or assembly line
- **Strategic Focus**: US reshoring and manufacturing reindustrialization
