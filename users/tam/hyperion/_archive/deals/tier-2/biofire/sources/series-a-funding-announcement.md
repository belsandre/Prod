## Raw Content Summary

**Press Release - November 7, 2022**

Biofire announced $14 million Series A funding led by Founders Fund, marking the first venture capital-led investment into a firearms technology company. Co-investors include 10X Capital, Gaingels, Will Manidis, with participation from existing investors Draper Associates, Structure Capital, Service Provider Capital, and Gavin de Becker & Associates.

**Company Background**:
Biofire, founded in 2016 by CEO Kai Kloepfer, emerged from stealth mode in May 2022 after three years of development. The company developed the Biofire Smart Gun®, the first handgun built from the ground up with fully integrated, instantly unlocking, state-of-the-art biometric technology. The Smart Gun instantly unlocks for authorized users but remains secured from unauthorized persons.

**Market Opportunity**:
Gun-related incidents became the leading cause of death for American children and teens in 2020. The Smart Gun targets prevention of firearm accidents and suicides. A national poll found 55% of gun owners and 45% of all adults would be comfortable using a Smart Gun.

**Team**:
Biofire's team includes experts from aerospace, defense, medical, automotive, and firearm engineering backgrounds.

**Use of Funds**:
The Series A funding will complete product development and bring the Smart Gun to market.

**Investor Quote**:
Founders Fund expressed excitement to partner with Biofire to develop groundbreaking technology that could prevent tragic accidents and improve firearm safety while respecting Second Amendment rights.

**Significance**:
This funding marks a watershed moment for firearms technology, breaking a long-standing VC taboo on investing in the firearms industry. Founders Fund's involvement signals conviction in both the technology and market opportunity despite political and reputational risks.
