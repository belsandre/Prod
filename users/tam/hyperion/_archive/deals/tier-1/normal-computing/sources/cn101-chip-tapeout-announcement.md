# Normal Computing Thermodynamic Computing Chip Announcement

## Product Details

**CN101** is the world's first thermodynamic computing chip, successfully taped out by Normal Computing. It represents a physics-based ASIC designed to harness natural dynamics like fluctuations, dissipation, and stochasticity for efficient computation.

### Key Capabilities:
- **Linear Algebra & Matrix Operations**: Solves large-scale linear systems for engineering and scientific computing
- **Stochastic Sampling**: Implements proprietary Lattice Random Walk (LRW)-based sampling for probabilistic computations
- **Energy Efficiency**: Targets up to 1000× energy consumption efficiency on targeted AI and scientific workloads
- **AI Inference**: Accelerates diffusion models with low-latency, high-throughput performance

## Development Roadmap

- **2026**: CN201 for high-resolution diffusion models and expanded AI workloads
- **Late 2027/Early 2028**: CN301 for advanced video diffusion models

## Executive Quotes

**Faris Sbahi, CEO**: "In recent months, we have seen that AI capabilities are approaching a flattening curve with today's energy budgets and architecture..." (statement emphasizes thermodynamic computing's potential for next-decade scaling)

**Patrick Coles, Chief Scientist**: Vision includes demonstrating CN101 applications this year, achieving state-of-the-art performance with CN201 next year, and delivering significant improvements via CN301 in two years.

**Zach Belateche, Silicon Engineering Lead**: CN101 represents the first silicon demonstration of their architecture leveraging randomness and metastability for sampling tasks.

## Company Background

Founded in 2022 by Google Brain, Google X, and Palantir veterans, with offices in New York, San Francisco, London, and Copenhagen.
