## Raw Content Summary

**Press Release - July 18, 2024**

Marathon Fusion, a San Francisco-based company founded in 2023, announced the close of a $5.9 million seed round led by 1517 Fund and Anglo American, with participation from Übermorgen Ventures, Shared Future Fund, Malcolm Handley, and other leading investors. Including a prior ARPA-E CREATE award, the company has raised $6.9 million total.

**Technology**:
Marathon develops high-throughput fuel processing systems for the fusion power industry, enabling rapid recycling of fusion fuel (deuterium and tritium). The technology allows fusion plants to operate with reduced tritium inventory, lowering operational expenses and enabling smaller, more economical facilities.

**Customer Traction**:
The company has secured Letters of Intent with Commonwealth Fusion Systems and Helion Energy, two major fusion developers with combined funding exceeding $2.6 billion. Brandon Sorbom, Chief Science Officer at Commonwealth Fusion Systems, stated the technology is "one of these key enablers" for making fusion plants viable.

**Support**:
Marathon is backed by the US Department of Energy's ARPA-E program and the Breakthrough Energy Fellows program. Malcolm Handley, an investor, noted that fuel processing represents "one of a few critical engineering problems that really enable the fusion ecosystem."

**Current Status**:
Headquartered in San Francisco and actively hiring for multiple roles to expand the team developing and deploying tritium processing systems for commercial fusion plants.

**Significance**:
The seed round validates Marathon's positioning as critical infrastructure for the emerging fusion industry. LOIs from tier-1 fusion companies ($2B+ raised) demonstrate customer willingness to engage, though commercial deployment remains 5-10 years out given fusion industry timeline. Strong investor base (1517 Fund, Anglo American, Breakthrough Energy) provides capital and network to execute on long-term opportunity.
