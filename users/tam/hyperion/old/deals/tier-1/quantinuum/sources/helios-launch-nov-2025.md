## Raw Content

### Full Press Release

**NEW YORK, Nov. 5, 2025** -- Quantinuum, the world's largest integrated quantum company, today announced the commercial launch of Helios, the world's most accurate general-purpose commercial quantum computer, designed to accelerate quantum computing adoption by enterprises.

Helios is now available to customers through Quantinuum's cloud service and on-premise offering, marking a significant milestone in the commercialization of quantum computing technology.

**Technical Specifications:**

Helios features 98 physical qubits at 99.921% 2-qubit gate fidelity and 99.9975% 1-qubit gate fidelity. The system also delivers:
- 94 logical qubits (error detected) globally entangled with better than physical performance
- 50 logical qubits (error detected) in magnetism simulation
- 48 logical qubits (error corrected) with 99.99% state prep and measurement fidelity

The system utilizes a QCCD (Quantum Charged Coupled Device) ion trap that includes a first-of-its-kind commercial ion junction, enabling efficient qubit routing and scaling. Helios computes with 98 barium ions at a time, an upgrade from its predecessor H2 which used 56 ytterbium qubits.

**Software and Integration:**

A new, modern Python-based programming language called Guppy allows developers to seamlessly combine hybrid compute capabilities—quantum and classical—in a single program. Helios integrates with NVIDIA's GB200 processors via NVQLink for real-time error correction and hybrid quantum-classical computing.

**Launch Customers:**

Initial customers include:
- Amgen (drug discovery and biologics)
- BMW Group (fuel cell catalyst research)
- JPMorgan Chase (financial analytics)
- SoftBank Corp (organic materials and battery technology)
- BlueQubit (AI image recognition)

**Strategic Partnerships:**

Quantinuum expanded its partnership with NVIDIA, serving as a founding collaborator in the NVIDIA Accelerated Quantum Research Center. The integration of Helios with NVIDIA's GB200 processors enables real-time error correction and supports the development of hybrid quantum-classical applications.

Additionally, Quantinuum announced a partnership with Singapore's National Quantum Office to establish an R&D center and deploy Helios locally, expected in 2026, to enable researchers to explore applications in computational biology.

**Leadership Statements:**

Dr. Rajeeb Hazra, President and CEO of Quantinuum, stated: "The next computing inflection point starts today. For the first time enterprises can access a highly accurate general purpose quantum computer to drive real world impact. Helios represents years of scientific advancement and engineering excellence, positioning us to deliver on the promise of quantum computing."

Jensen Huang, CEO of NVIDIA, commented: "Quantum computing is poised to be the next great leap in computing, and the path to useful quantum computing at scale will require hybrid quantum-classical systems. Our collaboration with Quantinuum brings together the best of both worlds."

Scot Baldry, Managing Director and CTO at JPMorgan Chase, said: "We have seen firsthand the transformative potential of Quantinuum's technology. Helios represents a significant step forward in our ability to tackle complex financial challenges."

**Additional Context:**

The announcement follows Quantinuum's selection by the Defense Advanced Research Projects Agency (DARPA) as a contractor for Stage B of DARPA's Quantum Benchmarking Initiative, and the company's recent $600 million capital raise at a $10 billion pre-money valuation in September 2025.
