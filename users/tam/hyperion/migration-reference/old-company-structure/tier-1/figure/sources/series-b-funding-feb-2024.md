## Raw Content

SUNNYVALE, Calif., Feb. 29, 2024 /PRNewswire/ -- Figure, an AI robotics company building the world's first commercially viable autonomous humanoid robot, today announced the close of a $675M Series B funding round at a $2.6B post money valuation. Figure also announced a collaboration agreement with OpenAI to develop next generation AI models for humanoid robots.

The round included investments from Microsoft, OpenAI Startup Fund, NVIDIA, Jeff Bezos through Bezos Expeditions, Amazon Industrial Innovation Fund, Parkway Venture Capital, Intel Capital, Align Ventures, and ARK Invest, among others.

The partnership with OpenAI will accelerate Figure's development of advanced AI capabilities for its humanoid robots. Peter Welinder, VP of Product and Partnerships at OpenAI, stated: "We see a path with Figure to explore what humanoid robots can achieve when powered by highly capable multimodal models."

Jon Tinter, Corporate Vice President of Business Development at Microsoft, said: "Figure will have access to Microsoft's AI infrastructure and services to support the deployment of humanoid robots."

Brett Adcock, Founder and CEO of Figure, commented: "This investment by some of the world's leading technology companies and investors ensures that we are well-prepared to bring embodied AI into the world to make a transformative impact. The collaboration with OpenAI represents a significant milestone in our mission to create humanoid robots that can truly understand and interact with the world."

The funding will be used to scale AI training operations, expand robot manufacturing, grow the engineering team, and accelerate commercial deployments of Figure's humanoid robots.

About Figure:
Figure is pioneering the development of general-purpose humanoid robots designed to work alongside humans in a wide range of commercial and industrial applications. The company's vision is to create robots that can help address labor shortages and perform tasks that are unsafe, undesirable, or impossible for humans.
