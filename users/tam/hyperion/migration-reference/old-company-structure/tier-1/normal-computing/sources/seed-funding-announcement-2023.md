Normal Computing Raises $8.5M in Seed Funding to Enable AI Solutions For Critical Enterprise and Government Applications

NEW YORK, June 14, 2023 /PRNewswire/ -- Normal Computing today announced that it has raised $8.5 million in seed funding that closed in January. The funding round was spearheaded by Celesta Capital and First Spark Ventures, with notable participation from Micron Ventures.

Normal Computing was founded by former members of Google Brain, Palantir, and X (Alphabet's moonshot division). The team previously built core AI production systems for Alphabet and developed ML frameworks including TensorFlow Probability and TensorFlow Quantum.

The company is advancing its probabilistic AI technology designed to address reliability issues in large language models, including hallucinations and unpredictable errors, while maintaining auditability and transparency through open-source model implementations.

The seed funding will support research and development of the application development platform and enable enterprise adoption of generative AI in high-stakes applications.

Normal Computing is initiating pilots with Fortune 500 companies across semiconductor manufacturing, supply chain management, banking, and government agencies.

Nicholas Brathwaite of Celesta Capital stated: "AI must be reliable, transparent, and able to comprehend the limits of its own reasoning."
