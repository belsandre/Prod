## Executive Summary

Haiqu is a quantum computing software company founded in fall 2022 that develops optimization software to enhance the performance of near-term quantum hardware. The company raised a **verified $4M pre-seed round in June 2023** led by MaC Venture Capital, with participation from high-quality investors including Toyota Ventures and SOMA Capital.

The company's core value proposition is "Careful Software for Clumsy Hardware" - addressing the fundamental challenge of qubit instability and noise in current quantum computers through software optimization rather than hardware improvements. This hardware-agnostic approach positions Haiqu as middleware in the quantum computing stack.

**Key Strengths**:
- Strong founding team (Stanford + Max Planck/Weizmann backgrounds)
- High-quality institutional investors (MaC VC, Toyota Ventures, SOMA Capital)
- Hardware-agnostic technology approach (works across quantum platforms)
- Strategic positioning in quantum software layer (critical enabler for near-term quantum utility)
- Alignment with Hyperion's quantum thesis and Dillon's Quantinuum relationship

**Key Concerns**:
- Major data discrepancy between dataroom and public sources
- Limited public information about product traction or customers
- Very early stage (founded 2022, small team of ~8-12 people)
- Highly competitive and uncertain quantum computing market
- No independent verification of technical claims or performance improvements

  - **Mykola Maksymenko** (CTO): PhD-level researcher from Max Planck Society and Weizmann Institute, former R&D head at SoftServe Inc.

- **Incubator**: Creative Destruction Lab Quantum stream (Toronto)
- **Website**: https://haiqu.ai/

### Business Model
- **Target Market**: Enterprise quantum computing users in finance, chemistry, life sciences, and mobility sectors
- **Product**: Quantum software middleware that optimizes application execution on quantum hardware
- **Go-to-Market**: Enterprise B2B sales (demo-based, not self-service)
- **Technology Approach**: Hardware-agnostic software layer (works across different quantum computing platforms)

### Value Proposition
Haiqu addresses a fundamental bottleneck in quantum computing: current quantum hardware is "clumsy" (error-prone, unstable qubits, limited coherence times). Rather than waiting for hardware to improve, Haiqu develops "careful" software that extracts maximum value from imperfect near-term quantum processors. This includes:
- Reducing circuit depth (reported 30x reduction in some cases)
- Minimizing noise sensitivity
- Optimizing for specific hardware constraints
- Enabling practical applications on near-term quantum computers
