## Raw Content

[Press Release]

**NEW YORK, June 6, 2023** -- Haiqu, a quantum computing software company, today announced it has raised $4 million in pre-seed funding led by MaC Venture Capital, with participation from Toyota Ventures, SOMA Capital, u.ventures, SID Venture Partners, Roosh Ventures, and angel investors Paul Holland, Alexi Kirilenko, and Gordy Holterman.

Haiqu develops software designed to enhance quantum processor performance by addressing hardware limitations and noise sensitivity in near-term quantum systems. The platform operates across different quantum hardware types and aims to unlock practical applications in finance, chemistry, life sciences, and mobility sectors.

Richard Givhan (CEO) and Mykola Maksymenko (CTO) formed Haiqu through Toronto's Creative Destruction Lab Quantum stream in fall 2022. Givhan is a Stanford alumnus and former Executive-in-Residence at Mitsubishi Electric. Maksymenko was formerly a researcher at Max Planck Society and Weizmann Institute of Science and previously led R&D at SoftServe Inc.

"We're accelerating the timeline to practical quantum computing by developing novel software that can extract value out of clumsy near-term quantum hardware," said Richard Givhan.

Adrian Fenty of MaC Venture Capital stated: "Quantum computing will fundamentally change the way we are able to solve meaningful problems."

Jim Adler of Toyota Ventures noted: "Haiqu caught our attention with their optimization software that boosts quantum hardware to solve practical industry problems."

The $4 million in funding will support R&D and go-to-market activities as Haiqu works to bring its quantum software solutions to market.
